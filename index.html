How I design

Start with the learner's next reliable move. In technical subjects, momentum matters—early wins should be real, not shallow.

Sequence for understanding. I prioritize dependencies and misconceptions so practice builds fluency instead of reinforcing gaps.

Assess to create feedback, not just scores. Checks for understanding are designed to reveal what to do next—for the learner and the instructor.

Open to curriculum and content roles — especially quantitative learning, math, and technical upskilling.

About

I'm a math-focused instructional designer and curriculum developer — specializing in the subjects where clear sequencing and precise representation matter most: mathematics, quantitative reasoning, and technical content.

I've designed and delivered instruction across K–12 math, test prep, and adult upskilling — plus time in industry as a decision science analyst, where translating quantitative findings for non-technical audiences was part of the job. I bring the same standard to editorial work on mathematical content: notation should be unambiguous, representations should earn their place, and every design decision should reduce load on the reader.
