Portfolio

A selection of curriculum and instructional design work across K–12 mathematics, adult learning, and online course development.

My approach: Good instruction starts with the learner, not the content. Before I think about what to teach, I ask who the learner is, what they already know, and what understanding actually looks like for them.

I design for clarity over decoration — visuals and structure should reduce cognitive load, not add to it. And I sequence deliberately, because in technical and mathematical subjects, small wins early create the momentum to tackle harder things later.

About

I'm a curriculum designer and STEM educator with a throughline that has always pointed back to education — whether I was in a classroom, an analytics role, or writing a grant as an undergraduate.

My work sits at the intersection of subject-matter depth and instructional craft. A mathematics degree gives me fluency in the content; years of designing, building, and delivering instruction give me fluency in the learner. I've found that both matter equally, and that most learning struggles trace back to a gap in one or the other — content that's technically correct but inaccessible, or delivery that's engaging but imprecise.

I'm drawn to contexts where the stakes are real: adult learners making a career transition, high school students whose relationship with math will shape what they believe they're capable of, nursing students who need to calculate dosages correctly. These are learners who don't have the luxury of disengaging, and they deserve instruction designed with the same seriousness they bring to it.

Outside the classroom, I've worked as a decision science analyst — which taught me that making a finding clear to someone who didn't ask for it, and getting them to trust it enough to act on it, is its own discipline. That experience lives in how I design now: every visual choice, every sequence decision, every analogy I reach for is in service of the moment when it lands.
